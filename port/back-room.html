Back Room

fixed media · year: 2022

Developed under the mentorship of Mag.art. Mag.phil. Mag.rer.nat. Daniel Mayer.

Back Room is a musique concrète piece created as part of an exercise. It is a programmatic composition in which you, as the listener, descend into a hall found in the back of an office room. As you go deeper, the walls around you turn into flesh and organic matter. You are unsure of what awaits you on the other side.

Techniques

Reaper was used for the creation of this piece. Only techniques that could’ve been achieved with tape were used. This includes time stretching, reversing, looping, splicing and layering. Time stretching also affects pitch.

All audio samples were recorded using a ZOOM microphone. A large portion of the material came from various cooking sounds—tapping, cutting, sizzling, tossing, etc. I also used metallic sounds produced from bowing on various metal objects with the help of Ivar Roban Križić. Other notable sounds include chewing and spitting.
