Prepared shishi odoshi

altered space, sound installation · year: 2025

Prepared shishi odoshi is a sound installation that was assembled in my dorm bathroom. It’s an exploration of altering space and learning about the mechanics behind shishi-odoshi, a water fountain and a sound sculpture originating from Japan.

Deer Scarer (work in progress)

sound installation, sound sculpture · year: 2025

Developed under the mentorship of Univ.Prof. Dr.phil. Gerhard Eckel.

Deer Scarer refers to the before-mentioned shishi-odoshi. After observing the movement in real life, I decided to mimic it using an electronically powered solution. Instead of water filling on one end of a tipping tube, a slowly rotating gear motor creates the motion.

The mechanical structure is in place, and what is left now is to sonify it. Its sonification will be achieved using two methods. One of them will involve having a contact microphone on one end of the tube, which will be used as a trigger. There will be beads inside the tube that, depending on the rotating cycle, hit the contact microphone, creating a trigger and playing audio samples through the speaker mounted on the outside of the sculpture.

The other type of sonification will be done using a speaker mounted on one side of the tube and a microphone freely moving and being pulled by gravity. This will result in feedback that is equalized to create different tonal variations.

This sound sculpture will be presented in Nürnberg, Germany, at the end of May 2025.
