Move on

interactive sound installation · year: 2024

Developed under the mentorship of Mag.art. Veronika Mayer.

Move on is a visual, audible, and interactive installation. It’s a reaction to space, a susurrating kinetic structure prone to change. It showcases the emigration of media from physical to digital. Newspaper is used to visualize this movement, crumpled and then piled up like leaves. To give some rigidness, wood glue was applied to a small portion of the paper. Amplification is achieved using homemade contact microphones.

Media

Techniques

Structure created by crumpling newspapers. For stiffness, a portion of the paper was first glued one on top of another before being crumpled.

Contact microphones were created by using piezo plates and soldering them onto audio cables. They were then wrapped in newspapers and hidden around the installation. Microphones are connected to a mixer where level control and equalization is applied. The output is sent through 4 speakers powered by an amplifier.

In order to view the rest of the exhibition one must pass through the installation. The results are varying reactions and approaches. As time goes on, the installation changes and the physical volume is lost and crushed.
