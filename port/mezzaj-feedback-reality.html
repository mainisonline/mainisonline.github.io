MEZZAJ – Feedback Reality

collaborative, edited live recording, structured improvisation · year: 2022

Created by: Martin Simpson, Max Reiner, Emanuel Križić, Zlata Zhidkova, Artem Sivashenko, Joseph Böhm

MEZZAJ – Feedback Reality is an audio-visual improvisational performance by the MMEZAJ collective. It’s a system where both audio and video feedback coming from the performers interact and influence each other, creating a large feedback loop. The resulting improvised performance is an instant mix of different types of data taken from the surrounding environment in one stream.

Media

video edited by Zlata Zhidkova

Techniques

Two microphones, two electro-magnetic pick-ups, four TVs and a camera. Two people with microphones and sensors wander through the room and capture audio signals and electromagnetic fields from the loudspeakers and the four tube televisions. These are then further processed by computers and synthesizers and distributed through the speakers.

Meanwhile, the CRTs create a distorted image of reality: a camera is pointed at the 4 screens, which emit the video signal from the camera, while the signals are continuously processed and influenced by the audio signal – so the visual feedback is also a closed loop.

Each player has their station. Mine was a laptop running FL Studio. I had a speaker and a microphone I would manipulate to create different rumbling sounds. In addition, I had one of the electro-magnetic pick-ups that I would press on various devices. I also had a MIDI keyboard on which I played audio samples and used it to mix and control the signals received from other players. Manipulation was done using various audio effects found in FL Studio.

We followed a time-based score in order to create some type of control and structure.
